Phase 01: Pigment Fixation

Tattoo Aftercare Ritual

Healing: 2-4 Weeks

01

The Purify

Remove bandage after 3-5 hours. Wash gently with lukewarm water and mild, fragrance-free soap. Never scrub; use light circular motions with fingertips.

02

The Hydrate

Apply a breathable, ultra-thin layer of artist-approved ointment. The skin should remain matte. Excess moisture traps bacteria and can pull out ink.

03

The Shield

Avoid direct sunlight, swimming pools, and intense gym sessions for 14 days. Do not wear abrasive clothing over the new artwork.

Phase 02

The L.I.T.H.A Method

Leave It The Hell Alone.

01

Sterile Saline

Clean twice daily using sterile 0.9% saline spray only. Avoid alcohol, peroxide, ointments, or homemade salt mixtures. Minimal products ensure maximum healing precision.

02

Zero Movement

Do not rotate, twist, or slide jewelry. Movement disrupts the forming fistula and causes irritation bumps. Healing requires stability.

03

Pressure Control

Avoid sleeping directly on new piercings. Use a travel pillow for ear placements. Reduce friction and compression during the first 8 weeks.

Scheduled Downsize Required

Return within 4–8 weeks to shorten initial jewelry posts. This prevents swelling retention and long-term angle distortion.

Healing Milestones

Day 01 - 07

Acute Response

Localized heat, swelling, and "weeping" are normal. Your body is sealing the site. Keep it clean, keep it dry, and avoid any friction.

Week 02 - 04

The Flaking Phase

Tattoos will peel like a sunburn. Piercings may develop light "crusties." The itch will be intense—do not scratch. This is where most pigment is lost through picking.

Month 06 - 12

Maturation

Internal tissue is finally stable. Tattoos have settled into the deep dermis. Maintain longevity by using SPF 50 whenever in the sun.

Diagnostic
Red Flags

Most issues are simple irritations, but we take infections seriously. If you identify these symptoms, immediate action is required.

Radiating heat & streaks moving from site
Green/Yellow discharge with a foul odor
Persistent fever, chills, or dizziness
